Welcome

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Michigan State University since August 2014, where I direct the Intelligent Sensing and Mobile Systems Lab. I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Engineering and M.S. degrees in both Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from University of Southern California under the supervision of Alexander Sawchuk. I received my B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from Peking University. Before joining MSU, I was a Postdoctoral Associate with Tanzeem Choudhury at Cornell University.

My students and I work on topics at the intersection of mobile systems and machine intelligence, covering areas including wearable and mobile sensing systems, ubiquitous computing, cyber-physical systems, Internet of Things, and mobile health (mHealth). Please visit my Publications page to learn more about my research work.
