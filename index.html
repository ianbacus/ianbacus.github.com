I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Michigan State University, where I direct the Intelligent Sensing and Mobile Systems Lab. I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Engineering and M.S. degrees in both Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from University of Southern California under the supervision of Alexander Sawchuk. I received my B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from Peking University. Before joining MSU, I was a Postdoctoral Associate with Tanzeem Choudhury at Cornell University. My students and I work on topics at the intersection of mobile systems and machine intelligence, covering areas including mobile and wearable sensing systems, ubiquitous computing, mobile health (mHealth), and cyber-physical systems/Internet-of-Things. Please visit my Publications page to learn more about my research work.

01/2017: iSee

We are developing a system for tackling this campus mental health crisis, called iSee. When it's ready to be deployed, students who participate in the program will need to carry a smartphone and a smartwatch. Data collected by those devices are transmitted to, and analyzed by, a computer system. This lets the relatively few counselors keep better track of more students - extending service to more students in need. The Conversation, Smithsonian Magazine
